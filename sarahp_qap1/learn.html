GO

Imagine the pedals and chain are like a team working together to make the bike move.

When you push the pedals with your feet, they turn in a circle. The pedals are connected to the chain, so when the pedals move, the chain moves too.

Photo from Specialized

The chain wraps around some special wheels called gears. When the chain moves around the gears, it makes the back wheel of the bike turn. And when the back wheel turns, the bike moves forward!

So, the pedals, chain, and gears are all working together to help the bike go. The harder you pedal, the faster the bike goes!

STOP

Let’s think of the front and back brakes as two helpers, each doing a special job to stop your bike.

Front Brake

The front brake is like the strong helper. When you squeeze the front brake handle, it’s like telling the front wheel, "Hey, slow down!" The brake pads press against the front wheel’s rim and slow it down. The front brake can stop you faster because it’s in charge of most of the stopping power, but if you squeeze it too hard, it can make the bike tip forward a little. So, the front brake is strong, but you have to use it carefully!

Photo from Specialized
Back Brake

The back brake is more like the gentle helper. When you squeeze the back brake handle, the brake pads press against the rim of the back wheel. The back brake slows you down more smoothly and helps you keep control, so you don’t skid or tip over. It’s not as strong as the front brake, but it’s great for slowing down safely without making the bike wobble.

Photo from Specialized
Working Together

When you use both brakes together, the front brake does most of the hard stopping work, and the back brake helps keep things smooth and steady. It’s like using both hands to catch a ball—one hand might grab it, but the other helps control it. Using both brakes at the same time keeps you in control and stops your bike safely!

SHIFT
Photo by Laura Ockel on Unsplash

Let’s think of gears as different “helpers” that make riding your bike easier or faster, depending on where you're going.

What Are Gears?

Gears are like a system of wheels connected by the chain. Your bike has some gears by the pedals (front gears) and some near the back wheel (rear gears). The chain moves between these gears when you shift, changing how easy or hard it is to pedal.

Photo by Specialized
How Do Gears Help?

Imagine you’re walking up a steep hill. It’s tough, so you’d probably take smaller steps to make it easier. When you switch to a lower gear (a smaller front gear or a bigger rear gear), the bike makes pedaling easier, just like taking smaller steps. This is perfect for going uphill or when you’re tired.

Now imagine you’re going downhill or want to ride fast on flat ground. You can take bigger steps because it’s easier. When you switch to a higher gear (a bigger front gear or a smaller rear gear), it makes pedaling harder but helps you go faster—like taking bigger steps to move quickly.

Quick Summary

Lower gears = easier pedaling, good for hills (smaller front, bigger back).

Higher gears = harder pedaling, good for speed (bigger front, smaller back). So, gears are like different tools that help you pedal more easily when you need to, or go faster when you can!
